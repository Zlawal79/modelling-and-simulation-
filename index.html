Monte Carlo Evacuation Simulation

Emergency
Evacuation Risk Analysis

Traditional evacuation models assume a single fixed reaction time for all occupants. This simulator treats reaction time as a random variable sampled from a log-normal distribution — running 1,000+ independent Monte Carlo trials to produce a full probability distribution of evacuation outcomes.

Model Assumptions
All occupants know exit locations and walk directly to the nearest exit
Walking speed is constant and fixed per simulation run
Reaction time is the only per-trial source of randomness (log-normal default)
Exit throughput follows a FIFO queue with capacity Ce persons/second
Panic, cooperation, and herding behaviours are not explicitly modelled
